Our Histroy

The japenese Womens Leadership initiative (JWLI) was founded in 2006 by three visionary women in Boston, Massachusetts: Atsuko Toko Fish, Mary Lassen, and Catherine Crone Coburn. JWLI brings emerging women leaders, referred to as the fellows, from Japan to Boston to receive four weeks of hands-on experience and training with succsessful nonprofit organization in nonprofit managment and leadership development. During thier stay in Boston, the fellows will develop an action plan, which will serve as a step-by-step roadmap to make their dreams of social change a reality. After returning to Japan, the fellows are expected to make a difference in their communities based on thier action plans. The purpose of this program is for the fellows to share their knowledge and experience they acquired in Boston with other women and social sector leaders within Japan. The fish family foundation, opening in conjucntion with other Boston-based nonprofit organizations, is adminestering JWLI in partnership with Simmons collehe school of managment center for gender in organizations. In 2010. thanks to the generous funding provided by the U.S- Japan foundation JWLI expanded its vision to hold public forums with Japan, held in 2010,2012 and 2013,these public forums provided information about the nonprofit sector and the American approach to Philanthropy with hopes to inspire women to take leadership roles. The forum program reached hundreds of japenese citizens, advocating for their involvment in addressin the needs of japenese society

Who We Are

Atsuko Toko Fish retired as a U.S-japan cross-cultrial consultant, and is currently involved with various social innovative movements as a philanthropsit. She is also a trustee of the Fish Family foundation. In the wake of 3.11, Atsuko established the japenese Disaster Relief fund-Boston (JDRFB) with the Boston Foundation (TBF) and the Japan Society Boston(JSB) to support immediate and mid-term recovery in the affected regions of Tohoku, Atsuko visited Tohoku several times after the disaster to assessed and evaluted the needs ofo the people and community. In the two years the fund was active for , JDRFB raised approxmatley $1 million and distributed 24 grants to 19 organizations and projects working directly in Tohoku.it has been reported by grantees that JDRFB'S $1 million grants were leveraged to have a $6 million of economic impact.

FISH FAMILY FOUNDATION

The Fish family foundation(the foundation) is a private family foundation in Boston. The foundation was established in 1999 to formalize the family's tradition of resposnibility to improve its community and to share the joy of giving with future generations.The foundation currently focuses on aiding immigration, at-risk youth, and people struggling with mental health.

SIMMONS

In 2007, the second year of JWLI, the center of gender in organizations (CGO) at the Simmons School of the management in Bosoton became JWLI's institutional and acedemic partner. CGO was selected because of their unique expertise in the areas pertaining to gender and leadership, its program located in the first business school in the world designed specifically for women, and expertise in th area of nonprofit management.
